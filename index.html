South Australia Crime Visualisation (2011–2022)

By Matthew Brasacchio | FIT3179 Data Visualisation

What changed across a decade?

This dashboard explores how crime has evolved across South Australia between 2011 and 2022. We combine geography, category mix, a comparative radar view, and an age profile to show a clear story: hotspots concentrate around key urban areas, the mix leans heavily toward property and violent offences, and by 2022 we see a stronger youth fingerprint—especially in violent and property crime.

Where is crime concentrated?

The map and bar chart highlight suburb intensity. The year slider Illustrates Adelaide and adjacent hubs consistently dominate incident counts, while the Top-5 list shifts depending on year although Adelaide is the only consistent.

What types of crime define the trend?

This stacked view shows the composition of total incidents. Across the decade, property-related and violent offences stay dominant, while public order and drug offences fluctuate within a narrower band. The takeaway: the headline story is still driven by high-volume categories.

The decade of change

The radar chart compares total contribution by category (e.g., Property, Violent, Public Order, Drug, White Collar, Other). Longer spokes mean a larger share. In the data, property and violent spokes extend the furthest—clear evidence of their central role in overall incidence. It is clear that people are commiting more extreme crimes more frequently.

Note the subtle shape change across years: the polygon for 2022 stretches a little further on violent and property, reinforcing the story from the stacked bars. Meanwhile, white-collar, drug, and public order remain nearer the center, indicating lower relative volume.

Who is commiting the crimes?

Three heatmaps (2011, 2016, 2022) compare age groups against crime types using % of total. The pattern shows a growing share from youth—especially Under 18 and 18–25—in violent and property offences by 2022. Highlighting text and delving deeper, provide both percentage and total numbers of crimes committed. It is evidengt youth crime is a growing problem

Take away about Crime

Crime in South Australia remains anchored by property and violent categories and is geographically concentrated in major population centres. By 2022, the evidence suggests a sharper youth presence, particularly in violent and property offences. Together, these findings point to two levers: place-based strategies for persistent hotspots, and targeted prevention for younger cohorts at risk of escalation into serious harm.

Data Source: South Australia Police Crime Statistics  |  Visualisations created using Vega-Lite
